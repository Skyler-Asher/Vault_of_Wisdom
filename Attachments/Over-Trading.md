## 1. Overtrading

**Description:** Overtrading is taking too many trades without a clear plan. It often stems from emotional impulses (FOMO, greed, or desperation) and leads to poor decision-making and excessive risk. In practice, an overtrading intraday trader jumps into every minor move or repeatedly enters and exits, generating high costs and small random losses.  
**Underlying Trap:** The core trap is emotional—traders fear missing out on opportunities or feel “in action” when constantly trading. This FOMO/greed mindset overrides a structured strategy.

  
**Manifestation:** An overtrader will exceed their usual daily trade count, ignore or override rules, and often “chase” minor fluctuations. For example, a trader may impulsively buy a stock after a sudden spike or repeatedly scalp small gains, incurring transaction costs and erratic results.  


**How to Overcome:** Adopt strict discipline and structural limits. Define a written trading plan with clear entry/exit criteria and set daily limits (e.g. max 1–3 trades, 3% daily drawdown) to cap activity.Use tools like stop-loss orders and fixed position sizes (e.g. 1–2% risk per trade) to enforce rules. Track your emotions and trades in a journal to spot patterns; for instance, log every impulse trade and its trigger. Habitually take breaks (step away after each trade or upon hitting limits) to reset emotion. These steps curb the impulse to overtrade and restore discipline
