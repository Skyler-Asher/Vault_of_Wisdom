# 12 / 03 / 2026 – Thursday

Today I focused on fixing a major weakness of working in terminal-based AI sessions: conversations disappear too easily.

I built a bridge between the CLI and my Obsidian vault so every interaction is logged automatically. The system writes a clean, readable summary for humans while also storing the full raw session data underneath for later analysis.

The goal is simple: nothing gets lost anymore. Every session becomes part of a permanent knowledge base instead of a temporary chat.

## 📝 Daily Summary
> [!ABSTRACT] Summary of Achievements
> ✦ Today, we built a robust, crash-proof system that turns your Gemini CLI sessions into a permanent, readable journal in your Obsidian Vault.
>
> **What We Built**
> - **Real-time Logging:** We implemented an `AfterModel` hook that saves every interaction the moment it happens. This means your data is safe even if your terminal closes or your PC crashes.
> - **Intelligent Formatting:**
>     - **Daily Journal Header:** Every day starts with a clean `Date - Day` header and space for a human-readable "story" of the day.
>     - **Automated Summary:** A bulleted list of timestamps and topics at the top for quick scanning.
>     - **Toggleable Raw Data:** Every chat is stored inside a `<details>` block. It keeps your Obsidian notes tidy for reading while preserving the full, raw text for future analysis.
> - **Project-Specific Logic:** The setup lives in a local `.gemini` folder within your vault, so it only activates when you're working in your chat directory.
>
> **Files Created**
> 1. `obsidian_logger.py`: The "engine" that handles formatting and writing to your vault.
> 2. `.gemini/settings.json`: The configuration that triggers the logger automatically.
> 3. `GEMINI.md`: A permanent instruction file so any future Gemini session here knows exactly how the system works.
> 4. `12-03-2026.md`: Your first complete daily log, fully recovered from today's session and formatted in your preferred journal style.
