## 2. Revenge Trading

**Description:** Revenge trading is the urge to “make back” a loss immediately by taking aggressive new trades. It typically happens after a losing trade or series of losses. Traders act impulsively (often larger size or lower quality setups) hoping to recoup losses, which usually amplifies losses[businessinsider.com](https://www.businessinsider.com/day-trading-mistakes-how-to-trade-stocks-market-psychologist-2025-8#:~:text=Around%2070%25,be%20getting%20into%20the%20market)[tiomarkets.com](https://tiomarkets.com/es/article/9-psychological-trading-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them#:~:text=Revenge%20trading%20happens%20when%20a,single%20event%20in%20the%20market).  
**Underlying Trap:** The trap is an emotional reaction (anger/frustration) combined with fear of missing out (FOMO). After a loss, the trader becomes fixated on being “right” or getting even, overruling rational judgment[tiomarkets.com](https://tiomarkets.com/es/article/9-psychological-trading-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them#:~:text=Revenge%20trading%20happens%20when%20a,single%20event%20in%20the%20market). This is a classic loss aversion response: the pain of a loss creates an obsessive need to eliminate that loss immediately.  
**Manifestation:** A revenging intraday trader will override their strategy: doubling position size, abandoning stops, or taking low-quality trades. For example, after a stop-out, a trader might immediately re-enter the market on slim pullbacks or random breakouts, ignoring entry rules. This often leads to multiple consecutive losses (“throwing good money after bad”) and emotional spiral.  
**How to Overcome:** Enforce strict rules around losses. Before trading, set and honor a **maximum daily loss limit** (e.g. 3%). If hit, stop trading for the day[tiomarkets.com](https://tiomarkets.com/es/article/9-psychological-trading-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them#:~:text=To%20control%20this%20and%20create,from%20trading%20for%20some%20time). After any loss, pause and review; do **not** immediately re-enter out of anger. In practice, write down your trading rules and hold yourself accountable – if you find yourself deviating (e.g. revenge entering), get out of the market at once[tiomarkets.com](https://tiomarkets.com/es/article/9-psychological-trading-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them#:~:text=To%20control%20this%20and%20create,from%20trading%20for%20some%20time). Use a journal to review each losing trade dispassionately. Over time this reflection breaks the emotional cycle: you learn to accept individual losses as part of the game and move on to the next valid setup rather than impulsively “chasing” losses[tiomarkets.com](https://tiomarkets.com/es/article/9-psychological-trading-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them#:~:text=Be%20patient%20and%20let%20the,happened%20instead%20of%20chasing%20losses).
